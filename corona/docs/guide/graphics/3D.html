2.5D/3D: Perspective, depth, and all that.

This guide will explain how you can achieve interesting 3D effects in Corona.

While Corona is not a general 3D engine, Corona's 2.5D engine allows you to simulate effects that appear to be three-dimensional (3D) without the complexity of working in 3D.

Background

In the Renaissance, Filippo Brunelleschi demonstrated the geometrical method of perspective that is still used today. This method solved the problem of how to represent three dimensional objects on a 2D plane.

This is the basis for how 3D computer graphics are represented on the computer screen. In essence, your eye is tricked into believing that what is rendered on a flat screen is actually 3D.

Perspective

Corona offers the ability to achieve perspective in an intuitive and simple manner.

Quadrilateral distortion

All [rectangles][api.type.RectObject] can be distorted into general quadrilaterals (quads). Each corner has an associated (x,y) property that specifies how the corner of the rectangle should be distorted. By default, these properties are (0,0), meaning there is no offset.

3D without the Z-axis

All Corona objects appear on the Z=0 plane. Objects follow the painter's model, that is objects that are drawn first appear below objects that are drawn later.

When you distort an image, it looks like it's placed in a 3D world, but it's actually all in the same plane. Thus, all of the standard object ordering rules will continue to be honored!

In the following video, a 3D flip effect is achieved using two images. In the foreground are swimming fish. In the middle of the flip transition, you can also notice a background aquarium image.

Nothing special is done to achieve this effect. You just place objects in the usual Corona fashion.

Billboarding

In the Corona graphics model, billboarding is available toll-free in Corona. Just create a group and make sure it's the top-most by calling group:toFront().

Super Nintendo Mode 7

In Mode 7, you could only place the background in perspective. In Corona, you can distort any rectangle to make the image appear in perspective.

Depth

Scaling along the Z-axis

In Corona, you can simulate depth by scaling objects as they move closer or further from the viewer.

This can be used to create the illusion of relative depth, e.g. a series of trees some that are larger than others.

Scrolling

The ability to place images in the background is toll-free in Corona. Just make sure your background image is drawn first (or you can move it to the back via object:toBack())

Consequently, horizontal scrollers, side scrollers, and bi-directional scrollers are all easily achievable in Corona.

Parallax

Parallax is used to provide a sense of "distance" between the camera and moving objects that recede back into the simulated space.

Learn more in our Parallax Tutorial

Skybox/Skydome

In the Corona graphics model, the effect you achieve using the skybox/skydome technique is available toll-free in Corona. Just create a group and make sure it's the back-most by calling group:toBack().
